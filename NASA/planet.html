Surface

Venra is covered by a deep ocean that is between 5 to 100 kilometers deep. There are no large land areas or continents, just small volcanic islands scattered here and there, formed by underwater volcanic activity.

Light

Little to no sunlight reaches the ocean depths. The surface receives faint red light due to the star’s lower output, forcing the planet to depend on chemosynthesis instead of photosynthesis for life to thrive.

Size

: Slightly larger than the Earth, with a diameter of 14,000 km

Location

0.2 astronomical units away from its red dwarf, in the habitable zone. Approximately 29 light-years away from Earth with an orbital period of about 40 Earth days (close orbit due to the dimness of the red dwarf).

T W I L I G H T

ZONE
5-20 KM down

Monster crabs

These massive, clawed creatures scuttle around the vent areas, feeding on the bacteria that coat the rocks. Monster Crabs can grow as big as a car and have thick, armored shells to protect them from the intense heat and chemicals near the vents.

Fire Eels

These slender and serpent-like creatures get their name from their glowing orange and red stripes. Fire Eels feed on the smaller creatures living near the vents and are quick hunters.

Floating Jelly Towers

Imagine jellyfish, but these are huge—bigger than a house! These massive jelly-like creatures drift around lazily and instead of hunting, they feed by filtering the water around them for bacteria and tiny particles. They glow softly in the dark water, like giant floating lanterns.

D U S K Y

ZONE
20-30 Km Down

Lush corals

Near the vents, where it’s still warm but not scalding, are corals which grow in clusters. They are colorful, tentacled creatures and they wave in the water, waiting for food to float by. Their bright, glowing colors stand out in the dark.

Slick eels

They are slender and scaleless creatures, quite similar to those on Earth, except they move smoothly in the water without causing a single sound. They feed on the small creatures near the vents, and without much light and no noticeable sound, they may as well be invisible.

Ghost Rays
